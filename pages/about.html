About This Project

The Media Literacy Quiz was created for the UNESCO Hackathon to help raise awareness about misinformation, fake news, and how to identify trustworthy sources.

Our goal is to empower people, especially students and young audiences, to think critically about the information they consume online.

What You’ll Learn

  • How to recognize fake news headlines
  • Spotting bias in articles
  • Understanding reliable sources
  • Practicing safe online sharing

Team

This project was built by beginners in programming as part of the UNESCO Hackathon. We believe media literacy is a key skill for today’s digital world.

About the Fake News Quiz

Why This Website?

    Ever wondered if the news you read online is real? With social media buzzing nonstop, spotting fake headlines isn’t easy. Our quiz turns media literacy into a fun challenge — helping you train your brain to spot misinformation quickly and confidently!

🎮

How It Works

  • We show a headline — real or fake.
  • You decide: ✅ Real or ❌ Fake.
  • Instant feedback explains your choice.
  • Rack up points and see your name climb the leaderboard!

Our Hackathon Story

    We created this app at the UNESCO Hackathon 2025 with one goal: fight fake news in a fun way. Our secret? turning critical thinking into a game! Every headline you challenge sharpens your skills and gives you the superpower to question what you see online.
